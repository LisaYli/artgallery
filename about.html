Hi, I’m Suyapa, and I’m a mixed media mosaic artist. My journey in mosaic artistry had some very humble beginnings, at a beginner mosaic course I attended while living in America. However, I loved the experience, and I was hooked!

Since then, I have lived and breathed mosaic art, researching techniques and ideas, finding inspiration from mosaic artist such as Antonio Gaudi, Niki de Saint Phalle , Isaiah Zagar among others, immersing myself in the subject so that I can better express my ideas and vision within the medium.

When I create pieces today, I never have a plan, or a predetermined outcome of any kind, I work in an Intuitive Style, a continuation of my journey. In this way, creativity can flourish without constraint, the project evolves naturally, the spontaneity of an idea shaping the piece as it comes. I love working this way, I surrender myself to the process, the feeling, and let the piece unfold as it will.

The result is art that truly expresses my story, that encompasses the feelings and thoughts of the moment. My art is, in the truest sense, me.

The materials used are influential throughout the process, the texture and shape contributing to the story being told, and here recycled materials have had a significant impact. The creative process is an ever evolving one, from the materials to my own vision, I believe that my journey has still only just begun, and through new materials and different themes my work continues to adapt and evolve. Always on the move, my art constantly delivers something new.

My Process: Making something out of nothing, transforming something neglected into something to be admired, these are the things I am passionate about. I use a wheeled nipper to hand cut every piece of glass, porcelain, tile or other material you see in my work. Each piece is then position carefully hand to build the art, section by section. This direct method, every individual element being placed face up onto the substrate before grouting, blends with my approach, as I shape the design and feel inch by inch to produce the final look. Once everything is how I want it, careful cleaning and polishing produces a luster that highlights the mosaic at its best.
